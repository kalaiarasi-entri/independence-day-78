Fortresses of Freedom: Guardians of India's Independence

1. Vellore Fort, Tamil Nadu

Historic Importance: Vellore Fort is famous for the Vellore Mutiny of 1806, an early revolt against British rule. This uprising by Indian soldiers (sepoys) was one of the precursors to the larger 1857 War of Independence. The fort's role in this rebellion marked it as an important site in the history of India's struggle for freedom

2. Red Fort (Lal Qila), Delhi

Historic Importance: Although primarily a Mughal stronghold, the Red Fort became a symbol of the independence movement. The fort was a focal point during the 1857 uprising and later, in 1947, where the first Prime Minister Jawaharlal Nehru hoisted the Indian flag, marking India’s independence

3. Palakkad Fort, Kerala

Historic Importance: Also known as Tipu's Fort, Palakkad Fort played a strategic role during the Anglo-Mysore Wars. It was a stronghold of Tipu Sultan, who fought against British forces. The fort’s history is closely tied to the efforts to resist British expansion in the region

About India

India is one of the oldest civilizations in the world with a kaleidoscopic variety and rich cultural heritage. It has achieved all-round socio-economic progress since Independence. As the 7th largest country in the world, India stands apart from the rest of Asia, marked off as it is by mountains and the sea, which give the country a distinct geographical entity. Bounded by the Great Himalayas in the north, it stretches southwards and at the Tropic of Cancer, tapers off into the Indian Ocean between the Bay of Bengal on the east and the Arabian Sea on the west.

Subramania Bharati

ABout His early life and more info

Bharati was born to Brahmin parents Chinnaswami Subramania Iyer and Lakshmi Ammal in Ettayapuram. He lost his mother at the age of five and his father at the age of 16. From a very young age, Bharathi was well versed in poetry and music and was well equipped with languages like Sanskrit, English, Hindi, Telugu, French and Arabic, which earned him the title of “Bharathi”.To escape his arrest, Bharati fled to the French ruled Pondicherry. While there, Bharati edited and published the weekly Journal India and Vijaya, which the British banned to suppress his nationalist outputs.Bharati was gravely injured by this attack and succumbed to his injuries on the 12th of September 1921, at the age of 38

Mahatma Gandhi

ABout His early life and more info

Monhandas Karamchand Gandhi was born in the small town of Porbandar, on the west coast of India, on October 2 1869.He was one of the national leaders who fought for the Indian Independence. In fact, he was the one who led the successful campaign for India’s Independence from British rule. Gandhi was a student of law, but he gave up his profession and chose to fight for his nation. According to him, “An eye for an eye will only make the whole world blind.” He followed non-violence and believed that violence was not the answer to everything.Gandhi was called ‘Mahatma’ for a reason. He was a great soul in the eyes of his fellow Indians. His continuous efforts and perseverance are what brought all the leaders and people together to stand against the British. He made everyone believe that, together, they could win their country back.

Bhagat Singh

ABout His early life and more info

Bhagat Singh was born into a Sikh family in 1907 in Lyallpur District, present-day Pakistan.Bhagat Singh is an important figure in the history of the Indian freedom movement. He was a revolutionary leader who was executed by the British.Although there have been many leaders from the revolutionary mould, Bhagat Singh's name is always first quoted when talking about revolutionary freedom fighters of India.The youth of India still draw a tremendous amount of inspiration from Bhagat Singh. He was voted the “Greatest Indian” in a poll by the Indian magazine India Today in 2008, ahead of Subhas Chandra Bose and Mahatma Gandhi. During the centenary of his birth, a group of intellectuals set up an institution named Bhagat Singh Sansthan to commemorate him and his ideals
